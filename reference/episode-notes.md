## Origin

The bit starts from a WikiHow article about attending your first rave. Justin references
Plato's Cave, Griffin combines it with a rave concept, and says "I only want to talk about
this" — which kicks off the 54-minute uninterrupted riff.

The brothers describe it as "the single most ambitious business idea of all time."

---

## The Concept

A 10-level rave hidden inside a Container Store. Each room escalates in intensity,
connected by airlocks and bathrooms. Patrons progress through rooms by proving their
"party level." It's free. It's carbon neutral.

### Entry
- 30-question questionnaire to determine starting level
- Blood pressure and pulse checks to calibrate intensity placement
- Travis: "You might say five, but we look at your pulse and we're like, 'No, this is a
  level two pulse.' If we drop you in five, you're gonna get the bends."
- You can ascend to higher levels if spiritually aligned, or descend if overwhelmed
- "Don't ascend too quickly. That's hubris."
- Also: resume/work experience check (Container Store staffing pipeline)

### PLUR Principles
Peace, Love, Unity, Respect — the rave ethos. ("We're PLURs, not WHDDs")

### The Circular Loop (Prestige)
Justin explicitly proposes: "Is it a circle? Like, do you get out of 10 and there's a door
that says 11, right. And you go in and it's room one again. But you're changed."

## Room-by-Room Breakdown

### Room 1 — The Container Store
The entry point. The "cave." You're just in a Container Store. This is Level 1.
Partnership with The Container Store — "going to The Container Store is like going to
a level one rave." ~30 seconds to sprint through.

### Room 2 — The Cloister of Confidence
The warehouse of the Container Store. You must be confident enough to rave through
while employees tell you "Please go. This is The Container Store. This is not the rave."
You have to keep raving through that. Risk of trespassing charges. Griffin: "not just
louder" — it's a completely different vibe. ~1-3 minutes. This room "circles back" to
Room 8 thematically.

### Room 3 — Dry Rub Wings (Snack Checkpoint 1)
Buffalo Wild Wings DoorDashed in. Dry rub only — no sauce yet. First of Travis's
snack checkpoint system (Rooms 3, 6, and 9). You can't take wings with you.
Non-pressurized exit available here for people who just came for the wings.
Vegan options available (Impossible Nuggets). ~15-30 minutes.

### Room 4 — The Best Rave of Your Life
"The best, fucking hardest rave you've ever been to in your whole life." The most
intense rave experience. Griffin: "At the end of this, your consciousness is going to
exist on a level that is unthinkable to the normal sort of id and ego, and super ego."
Could trap people here for hours — they might think "I can't get better." ~2-3 hours.

### Room 5 — Barometric Pressure
Intense barometric pressure. It feels like descending — "like you're lowering down in
the ocean, your ears are gonna pop." Three proposed versions: psychological pressure,
submersible room, or just pressurize it like a plane. Justin: "You're gonna be crying at
movies, it'll be just like a plane." Airlock required between 4 and 5 (bathroom serves
as airlock). ~10-15 minutes pressurization. Layers 5-10 are pressurized — if anyone
opens a window, everyone gets launched out.

### Room 6 — Sauce on Six (Snack Checkpoint 2)
Buffalo Wild Wings again, but now with SAUCE. The wet wings are "so much faster"
because you're starving from the pressure. Exit also available here for the "real wild
folks" who just came for sauced wings underground. The sauce brings you back from
the barotrauma. Griffin: "After the barotrauma, they're gonna taste like the best
fuckin' wings." Also a ghost kitchen / DoorDash window.

### Room 7 — Traps
All traps. Trap music. Trap drums. People with big trap muscles watching you. Trapezes
to swing over the traps. Everyone dressed like "music videos from the first Panic at the
Disco album" — Gothic circus aesthetic. Travis proposes a Gothic ringmaster (Griffin
vetoes: "Now it's feeling like Eyes Wide Shut"). The band Trapt is on retainer — dropped
in a random room each day. Staind-brand earplugs to block out Trapt's music.
Griffin: "People are gonna be so fucked up and bedraggled by the time they reach
room seven." Airlock between 7 and 8. If you know Latin, bring a prism, or are a WWI
scholar, you can speed-run this room. ~1 hour average.

### Room 8 — The Final Deception / Cloister of Deceit
**NOT** a second cloister of confidence. It's a fake Container Store — "this one can't be
a real Container Store. The second one can't be, because why would there be two
Container Stores so close to each other?" It's staffed by actors selling containers to
actors ("a can't-tainer store"). They want you to think you failed — "There is no level
eight, nine or 10. This is all we've made." One receipt in one till says "The Cantainer
Store" — find it and you've solved it, you're ready for level 9. Griffin: "I want the
deception to last a lot longer." Justin wants weeks. They settle on ~1 hour / 4 hours
of deception. Deep underground at this point — "How would that work? We're deep
underground at this point, Travis."

### Room 9 — Nirvana / Transcendence (Snack Checkpoint 3)
"Nine is nirvana, baby. Nine is the real best — nine is the best, objectively, the best
layer. Nine is the best rave." The transcendent experience — "it's like when you go to
a rave, it is the transcendent experience that you want this to be." You don't want to
leave nine. "You can't get out of nine until you don't want to go to 10." The fun one.
Also: archivist wing (people who've devoted themselves to scholarship and maintenance),
long tunnel to Room 10 with pictures of your life/greatest moments, archivists'
bathrooms (one has Trapt in it). Griffin: "eternal afterlife pleasure garden."
Justin: 45 minutes tops for him personally.

### Room 10 — The Candy King
A dark room. One person sits on a throne wearing a candy crown of beads. "Just hard
candy regalia." A single spotlight illuminates you when you walk in. He looks down at
you and says either:
- **"Not yet"** → Booted right back to Room 1
- **He disintegrates** → You become him / you become a Plato's Rave franchisee

Only one person in Room 10 at a time. "So, you better be raving harder than they are.
Or you're dead. I mean, not dead."

Travis's alternative: opening door 10 depressurizes everything, the whole thing implodes
on itself. (Griffin vetoes: "People will get hurt doing that.")

## Other Key Details

- **It's Free:** No admission charge
- **300-Key System:** 300 keys must all turn simultaneously for the music to drop.
  Like a THX sound intro stretched over ~20 minutes.
- **DJs:** 10 rooms, 10 DJs, "10 varying degrees of vibe and intensity"
- **Drinks:** Domestic beer and well drink tickets provided
- **Carbon Neutral:** Every time someone gets evaporated in Room 10, they plant one tree
- **No Parking:** The rave does not have parking
- **No Drugs:** "No drugs at all." "Like really, no drugs at all." Barely water.
  Container Store has one container filled with drugs they make you throw away.
- **Bathrooms:** On every layer, clearly marked. Punch card system (3 uses) or
  attendant counting "one, two, three, you're done." Between rooms 4 and 8
  they serve as airlocks/Switzerland zones — "not part of the rave." No talking.
  No sleeping in the bathrooms.
- **Franchise Model:** Travis reveals Trav Nation has already installed Plato's Raves
  "in every Container Store in the contiguous US." When you beat Room 10, you
  become a franchisee.
- **Duration:** ~12 hour experience total. Room 1: 30s, Room 2: 1-3min, Room 3:
  15-30min, Room 4: 2-3hrs, Room 5: 10-15min pressurization, Room 6: fast,
  Room 7: ~1hr, Room 8: 1-4hrs, Room 9: 45min, Room 10: variable.
- **Theme Song:** "My Life Is Better With You" by Montaigne — "a level nine slapper"

## Notes for Game Adaptation

This file is reference material — the game design doc is in `design/game-design.md`.

### Key Lore for Mechanics
- **Pressure/Pulse system:** The entry questionnaire checks blood pressure and pulse
  to determine your starting room. Travis explicitly says you can't ascend too fast or
  you'll "get the bends." This maps naturally to game mechanics where different rooms
  increase/decrease internal stats (pressure, pulse, vibe) at different rates, and being
  in the optimal room for your current state yields better gains.
- **Circular loop:** Justin explicitly proposes the circular structure — "you go in and
  it's room one again. But you're changed." This is the prestige mechanic.
- **Ascending/descending:** "Don't ascend too quickly. That's hubris." — pacing matters.
- **Speed running:** Griffin mentions that knowing Latin, chess notation, or bringing a
  prism lets you speed-run the trap room. Room-specific buffs/knowledge carry over.
- **The Cantainer Store puzzle:** Finding the receipt is a solvable challenge, not just
  time-gated progression.
- **Archivists:** People who chose to stay in service of the candy king rather than
  challenge him. Could be an NPC/upgrade system.
