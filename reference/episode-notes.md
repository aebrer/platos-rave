## Origin

The bit starts from a WikiHow article about attending your first rave. Justin references
Plato's Cave, Griffin combines it with a rave concept, and says "I only want to talk about
this" — which kicks off the 54-minute uninterrupted riff.

The brothers describe it as "the single most ambitious business idea of all time."

---

## The Concept

A 10-level rave hidden inside a Container Store. Each room escalates in intensity,
connected by airlocks. Patrons progress through rooms by proving their "party level."

### Entry
- 30-question questionnaire to determine starting level
- Blood pressure and pulse checks to calibrate intensity placement
- You can ascend to higher levels if spiritually aligned, or descend if overwhelmed

### PLUR Principles
Peace, Love, Unity, Respect — the rave ethos. ("We're PLURs, not WHDDs")

---

## Room-by-Room Breakdown

### Room 1 — The Container Store
The entry point. The "cave." You're just in a Container Store. This is Level 1.

### Room 2 — The Cloister of Confidence (Container Store Warehouse)
You must be confident enough to risk trespassing charges at the Container Store to find
the passage to Room 3. This takes nerve — you're going into the warehouse.

### Room 3 — Dry Rub Wings
Buffalo Wild Wings are DoorDashed in. Dry rub only. First snack checkpoint (Travis's
snack checkpoint system: Rooms 3, 6, and 9).

### Room 4 — Escalation
Rave intensity escalates. Details fuzzy — transitional room between wings and pressure.
Airlock between Rooms 4 and 6.

### Room 5 — Barometric Pressure
Intense barometric pressure. Revisions proposed: underground? underwater? purely
psychological? Justin suggests a six-month training period in the intense pressure.

### Room 6 — Sauce on Six
Buffalo Wild Wings again, but now with SAUCE. Transitional space between Room 5's
pressure and Room 7's traps. The sauce brings you back — if you don't survive Room 7,
the sauce in Room 6 helps you return to Room 1.

### Room 7 — Traps (feat. Trapt)
Actual traps, plus the band Trapt plays here. The traps are harder now that you're full
of saucy wings from Room 6. Staind-brand earplugs available to block out Trapt's music.
The band prays for Room 3 or 6 assignment to avoid starvation.
Airlock between Rooms 7 and 8.

### Room 8 — Second Cloister of Confidence
Counterpart to Room 2. A Container Store employee confronts you — the brother of the
one who met you in Room 2.

### Room 9 — Third Snack Checkpoint
Travis's snack checkpoint system. Details less defined than 3 and 6.

### Room 10 — The Throne
A single person sits on a throne wearing a kandi crown (elaborate candy-themed rave
regalia). Only one person in Room 10 at a time.

When you enter, the king assesses your party level:
- **"Not yet"** → You are transported back to Level 1
- **You surpass his level** → He disintegrates and you become him

Becoming the king also makes you a Plato's Rave franchisee.

## Other Details

- **300-Key System:** 300 keys must all turn simultaneously for the music drops. Like a
  THX sound intro stretched over ~20 minutes.
- **DJs:** Each room has a DJ with "varying degrees of vibe"
- **Drinks:** Domestic beer and well drink tickets provided
- **Carbon Neutral:** Every time someone gets evaporated in Room 10, they plant one tree
- **No Parking:** The rave does not have parking
- **Franchise Model:** Travis reveals Trav Nation has already installed Plato's Raves
  "in every Container Store in the contiguous US"
